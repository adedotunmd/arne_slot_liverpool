## Examining Liverpool's 2025/26 Collapse Through Historical Context

## Overview

Liverpool's 2025/26 season ended in disappointment and ultimately led to the dismissal of Arne Slot. However, poor seasons are not unprecedented in Liverpool's recent history.

This project investigates whether the club's decline during the 2025/26 campaign was severe enough to justify a managerial change. Using historical league performance and injury data, the analysis compares Liverpool's 2025/26 season against previous dip seasons from the Klopp era to determine whether the downturn was an anomaly or part of a recurring pattern.

Rather than evaluating the season in isolation, this analysis places it within the broader context of Liverpool's performance over the last decade.

**Key Question:** Was Arne Slot's dismissal justified, or was Liverpool experiencing a decline similar to downturns the club had previously recovered from?

---

## Objectives

- Compare Liverpool's 2025/26 season against previous dip seasons.
- Identify where 2025/26 ranks among Liverpool's weakest campaigns.
- Examine whether injuries played a significant role in the decline.
- Evaluate whether the scale of the downturn was unprecedented.
- Provide data-driven context to the decision to dismiss Arne Slot.

---

## Analysis Questions

1. How does Liverpool's 2025/26 points tally compare to previous seasons?
2. Was the decline significantly worse than previous downturns?
3. Did Liverpool suffer an unusual injury crisis?
4. How does player availability compare with previous dip seasons?
5. Does the evidence support the decision to sack Arne Slot?

## Methodology

The analysis combines:

- Liverpool Premier League points totals by season
- Total days lost to injury
- Number of injured players per season
- Historical league performance trends

The 2025/26 season is benchmarked against Liverpool's previous dip seasons to determine whether the downturn represented a new low or followed an established historical pattern.

## Key Findings

### The 2025/26 Season Was Not Liverpool's First Major Dip

Liverpool have experienced several significant downturns over the last decade. The analysis compares the magnitude of each decline to determine whether 2025/26 stands apart from previous struggles.

### Injury Burden Increased During Dip Seasons

Periods of underperformance were often accompanied by increases in both total injury days and player unavailability.

### Context Matters

Evaluating a manager based solely on a single season can be misleading. Comparing 2025/26 with previous downturns provides a more balanced assessment of whether the club's response was proportionate.

### The Verdict

The data allows readers to decide whether Liverpool's decline under Slot represented a uniquely poor season or a level of performance that had historical precedent within the club's recent history.
